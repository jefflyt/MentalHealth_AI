CHAT (Community Health Assessment Team) Services for Singapore Youth

CHAT is a mental health service specifically designed for young people aged 16-30 in Singapore. It provides free and confidential mental health support, making it easily accessible for youth who may be experiencing mental health challenges.

What is CHAT?
The Community Health Assessment Team (CHAT) is a collaboration between the Institute of Mental Health (IMH) and various community partners to provide early intervention and support for youth mental health issues.

Key Features:
- FREE mental health services
- No appointment necessary (walk-in service)
- Confidential and youth-friendly environment
- Staffed by qualified mental health professionals
- Early intervention approach
- Community-based locations for easy access

Who Can Use CHAT Services?
- Young people aged 16-30 years
- Singapore citizens and permanent residents
- Those experiencing mental health concerns
- Family members seeking guidance about a loved one's mental health
- Anyone wanting to learn more about mental health

Services Provided:
1. Mental Health Screening and Assessment
   - Comprehensive evaluation of mental health status
   - Risk assessment and safety planning
   - Identification of mental health conditions

2. Brief Counseling and Support
   - Individual counseling sessions
   - Crisis intervention and support
   - Coping skills development
   - Emotional support and validation

3. Referrals and Care Coordination
   - Referrals to appropriate mental health services
   - Connection to IMH specialist services when needed
   - Liaison with other healthcare providers
   - Community resource connections

4. Mental Health Education
   - Information about mental health conditions
   - Guidance on self-care and wellness
   - Resource materials and educational content

How to Access CHAT Services:
1. Walk-in Service: No appointment needed, simply visit during operating hours
2. Phone Consultation: Call 6493-6500 for immediate support or information
3. Online Resources: Visit the CHAT website for mental health information and resources

What to Expect:
- Warm, welcoming, and non-judgmental environment
- Initial conversation with CHAT staff to understand your concerns
- Comprehensive but relaxed assessment process
- Discussion of available options and next steps
- Information about additional resources and support

Benefits of CHAT:
- Early intervention can prevent mental health issues from worsening
- Free service removes financial barriers to mental health care
- Youth-friendly approach makes it comfortable for young people
- Community-based locations are convenient and accessible
- Professional staff provide quality mental health support

Follow-up and Continuing Care:
- CHAT can provide ongoing brief counseling
- Referrals to IMH or other specialized services when needed
- Connection to community support groups and resources
- Regular check-ins and follow-up as appropriate

Important Information:
- All services are strictly confidential
- Parental consent not required for those 16 and above
- Bring NRIC or identification document
- Service is available in English and other languages with interpreter support
- CHAT staff are trained mental health professionals

For more information or immediate support, call CHAT at 6493-6500 or visit any CHAT center during operating hours.
