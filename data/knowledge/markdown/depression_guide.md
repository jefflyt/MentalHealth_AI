# Depression Guide

## What is Depression?

Depression (Major Depressive Disorder) is a common and serious mental health condition that negatively affects how you feel, think, and handle daily activities.

### Key Characteristics

- **Persistent sad mood** lasting at least 2 weeks
- **Loss of interest** in activities once enjoyed
- **Significant impact** on daily functioning
- **Physical symptoms** affecting sleep, appetite, and energy

---

## Types of Depression

### 1. Major Depressive Disorder (MDD)
Severe symptoms that interfere with work, sleep, studying, eating, and enjoying life. Can occur once or multiple times.

### 2. Persistent Depressive Disorder (Dysthymia)
Depressed mood lasting at least 2 years. May include major depressive episodes.

### 3. Postpartum Depression
Occurs after childbirth, more severe than "baby blues." Includes extreme sadness, anxiety, and exhaustion.

### 4. Seasonal Affective Disorder (SAD)
Depression during winter months when there's less natural sunlight. Usually improves in spring/summer.

### 5. Depression with Psychotic Features
Severe depression accompanied by psychotic symptoms like delusions or hallucinations.

---

## Signs and Symptoms

### Emotional Symptoms
- Persistent sad, anxious, or "empty" mood
- Feelings of hopelessness or pessimism
- Irritability
- Feelings of guilt, worthlessness, or helplessness
- Loss of interest in hobbies and activities

### Physical Symptoms
- Decreased energy or fatigue
- Difficulty sleeping or oversleeping
- Changes in appetite or weight
- Aches, pains, headaches without clear cause
- Slowed movements and speech

### Cognitive Symptoms
- Difficulty concentrating or making decisions
- Memory problems
- Thoughts of death or suicide
- Suicide attempts

---

## Causes and Risk Factors

### Biological Factors
- Brain chemistry imbalances
- Genetics and family history
- Hormonal changes
- Chronic medical conditions

### Psychological Factors
- Trauma or stressful life events
- Low self-esteem
- Pessimistic thinking patterns
- History of other mental health disorders

### Environmental Factors
- Financial problems
- Relationship difficulties
- Loss of a loved one
- Work-related stress
- Social isolation

## Treatment Options in Singapore

### 1. Psychotherapy

**Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT)**
- Evidence-based approach
- Identifies and changes negative thought patterns
- Available at polyclinics, IMH, and private centers

**Interpersonal Therapy (IPT)**
- Focuses on relationships and communication
- Effective for depression triggered by life changes

**Mindfulness-Based Cognitive Therapy (MBCT)**
- Combines CBT with mindfulness practices
- Prevents relapse in recurrent depression

### 2. Medication

**Antidepressants**
- SSRIs (Selective Serotonin Reuptake Inhibitors)
- SNRIs (Serotonin-Norepinephrine Reuptake Inhibitors)
- Others as prescribed by psychiatrist

**Important Notes:**
- Takes 2-4 weeks to feel full effects
- Must be taken as prescribed
- Don't stop suddenly without doctor's guidance
- Side effects usually improve over time

### 3. Lifestyle Changes

- Regular exercise (30 minutes daily)
- Healthy sleep routine
- Balanced nutrition
- Social connection
- Stress management techniques
- Avoid alcohol and drugs

### 4. Support Services

**Institute of Mental Health (IMH)**
- 24/7 Helpline: 6389 2222
- Emergency services available
- Outpatient and inpatient care

**Polyclinics**
- Mental health screening
- Subsidized counseling
- Referrals to specialists

**Community Support**
- SAMH (Singapore Association for Mental Health)
- Silver Ribbon Singapore
- Support groups

## When to Seek Immediate Help

### Emergency Situations (Call 995 or go to A&E)
- ✋ **Suicidal thoughts or plans**
- ✋ **Thoughts of harming others**
- ✋ **Hearing voices or seeing things**
- ✋ **Unable to care for yourself**

### Crisis Helplines (24/7)
- **SOS (Samaritans of Singapore):** 1767
- **IMH Mental Health Helpline:** 6389 2222
- **National CARE Hotline:** 1800 202 6868

## Self-Help Strategies

### Daily Habits
1. **Maintain routine** - Regular sleep, meals, activities
2. **Set small goals** - Break tasks into manageable steps
3. **Stay active** - Even light exercise helps
4. **Connect with others** - Reach out to supportive people
5. **Avoid isolation** - Join groups or activities
6. **Limit alcohol** - Can worsen depression
7. **Practice self-compassion** - Be kind to yourself

### Coping Techniques
- **Journaling** - Express feelings and track mood
- **Mindfulness** - Stay present, reduce rumination
- **Gratitude practice** - Note 3 good things daily
- **Creative outlets** - Art, music, writing
- **Nature time** - Spend time outdoors

---

## Supporting Someone with Depression

### DO:
✅ Listen without judgment  
✅ Encourage professional help  
✅ Offer practical support  
✅ Be patient and understanding  
✅ Take suicide talk seriously  
✅ Stay in regular contact  

### DON'T:
❌ Say "just snap out of it"  
❌ Minimize their feelings  
❌ Give unsolicited advice  
❌ Force them to "cheer up"  
❌ Take their behavior personally  
❌ Ignore warning signs

## Prognosis and Recovery

Depression is **highly treatable**:
- 80-90% of people respond to treatment
- Combination of therapy and medication most effective
- Recovery is possible and common
- Relapse prevention strategies help maintain wellness

### Recovery Timeline
- **2-4 weeks** - Medication starts working
- **6-8 weeks** - Noticeable improvement
- **3-6 months** - Significant recovery
- **12+ months** - Continued treatment prevents relapse

## Cultural Considerations in Singapore

### Addressing Stigma
Mental health stigma remains a challenge in Asian cultures. Remember:
- Depression is a **medical condition**, not a weakness
- Seeking help shows **strength**
- Recovery is **possible** with proper treatment
- You're **not alone** - 1 in 7 Singaporeans affected
