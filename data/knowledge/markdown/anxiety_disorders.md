# Anxiety Disorders Guide

## Understanding Anxiety

Anxiety is a normal human emotion. However, when anxiety becomes excessive, persistent, and interferes with daily life, it may be an **anxiety disorder**.

### Normal Anxiety vs. Anxiety Disorder

| Normal Anxiety | Anxiety Disorder |
|----------------|------------------|
| Temporary worry about specific events | Excessive worry about many things |
| Proportional to the situation | Out of proportion to actual threat |
| Goes away after event passes | Persists for months (6+ months) |
| Manageable | Interferes with daily functioning |
| Motivating | Debilitating |

---

## Types of Anxiety Disorders

### 1. Generalized Anxiety Disorder (GAD)

**Characteristics:**
- Excessive worry about everyday things
- Difficulty controlling worry
- Lasts 6 months or more
- Accompanied by physical symptoms

**Common Worries:**
- Health, money, family
- Work performance
- Future events
- Minor matters

**Prevalence in Singapore:** ~2% of population

---

### 2. Panic Disorder

**Panic Attacks Include:**
- Sudden intense fear
- Peak within minutes
- Physical symptoms: racing heart, sweating, trembling, chest pain
- Fear of dying or losing control
- May occur "out of the blue"

**Panic Disorder:**
- Recurrent unexpected panic attacks
- Persistent worry about having more attacks
- Avoidance of situations that might trigger attacks

**Impact:** Can lead to agoraphobia (fear of places/situations)

---

### 3. Social Anxiety Disorder (Social Phobia)

**Characteristics:**
- Intense fear of social situations
- Fear of being judged, embarrassed, or humiliated
- Avoidance of social interactions
- Physical symptoms in social settings

**Common Feared Situations:**
- Public speaking
- Eating in front of others
- Meeting new people
- Being the center of attention
- Using public restrooms

**Cultural Note:** May be more common in collectivist Asian cultures due to emphasis on social harmony and "face"

---

### 4. Specific Phobias

**Common Types:**
- **Animal:** Spiders, snakes, dogs, insects
- **Natural environment:** Heights, storms, water
- **Blood-Injection-Injury:** Needles, medical procedures
- **Situational:** Flying, elevators, enclosed spaces
- **Other:** Choking, loud noises, costumed characters

**Criteria:**
- Marked, excessive fear of specific object/situation
- Immediate anxiety response
- Active avoidance
- Impairs functioning

---

### 5. Agoraphobia

**Fear of:**
- Public transportation
- Open spaces (parking lots, markets)
- Enclosed spaces (shops, theaters)
- Standing in line or crowds
- Being outside home alone

**Key Feature:** Fear of not being able to escape or get help if panic symptoms occur

---

### 6. Separation Anxiety Disorder

**Not just for children!** Can occur in adults

**Symptoms:**
- Excessive fear of separation from attachment figures
- Worry about losing loved ones
- Reluctance to be alone
- Nightmares about separation
- Physical symptoms when separation occurs

## Causes and Risk Factors

### Biological Factors
- **Genetics:** Family history increases risk
- **Brain chemistry:** Imbalances in neurotransmitters (serotonin, GABA, norepinephrine)
- **Brain structure:** Amygdala (fear center) overactivity
- **Medical conditions:** Thyroid problems, heart conditions

### Psychological Factors
- **Personality traits:** Perfectionism, neuroticism, behavioral inhibition
- **Thinking patterns:** Catastrophizing, black-and-white thinking
- **Past experiences:** Trauma, abuse, stressful life events
- **Learned behaviors:** Anxiety modeled by parents/caregivers

### Environmental Factors
- **Stress:** Work pressure, academic demands, financial problems
- **Life transitions:** Moving, job changes, relationship issues
- **Trauma:** Accidents, natural disasters, violence
- **Cultural factors:** High achievement expectations in Singapore

## Treatment Options

### 1. Psychotherapy

**Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT)**
- **Gold standard** for anxiety disorders
- Identifies and challenges anxious thoughts
- Gradual exposure to feared situations
- Develops coping strategies

**Sessions:** Typically 12-20 sessions  
**Effectiveness:** 60-80% improvement  
**Available at:** Polyclinics, IMH, private practices

**Exposure Therapy**
- Systematic desensitization
- Gradual confrontation of fears
- Prevents avoidance behaviors
- Highly effective for phobias, panic disorder

**Acceptance and Commitment Therapy (ACT)**
- Accepts anxiety rather than fighting it
- Focuses on values-based living
- Mindfulness techniques

---

### 2. Medication

**SSRIs (Selective Serotonin Reuptake Inhibitors)**
- First-line medication
- Examples: Escitalopram, Sertraline, Fluoxetine
- Takes 2-4 weeks to work
- Few side effects

**SNRIs (Serotonin-Norepinephrine Reuptake Inhibitors)**
- Examples: Venlafaxine, Duloxetine
- Alternative to SSRIs
- Effective for GAD

**Benzodiazepines**
- Fast-acting (minutes)
- For short-term use or acute panic
- Risk of dependence
- Examples: Alprazolam, Lorazepam

**Beta-Blockers**
- For physical symptoms (racing heart, trembling)
- Useful for performance anxiety
- Example: Propranolol

**Important:** Always take as prescribed by psychiatrist

---

### 3. Self-Help Strategies

**Breathing Techniques**
- **4-7-8 breathing:** Inhale 4 counts, hold 7, exhale 8
- **Box breathing:** Inhale 4, hold 4, exhale 4, hold 4
- **Diaphragmatic breathing:** Deep belly breaths

**Mindfulness and Meditation**
- Focus on present moment
- Observe thoughts without judgment
- Reduces rumination
- Apps: Headspace, Calm, Insight Timer

**Progressive Muscle Relaxation**
- Tense and release muscle groups
- Reduces physical tension
- Practice daily for best results

**Exercise**
- **30 minutes daily** recommended
- Reduces stress hormones
- Increases endorphins
- Any activity counts: walking, swimming, yoga

## Myths vs. Facts

| Myth | Fact |
|------|------|
| "Anxiety isn't a real illness" | Anxiety disorders are legitimate mental health conditions |
| "It's just stress, it will pass" | Anxiety disorders don't go away without treatment |
| "Medication is the only solution" | Therapy is equally or more effective |
| "I should be able to control it" | Anxiety disorders involve brain chemistry, not willpower |
| "Seeking help is weak" | Seeking help shows strength and wisdom |
