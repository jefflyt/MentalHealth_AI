Anxiety is a natural response to stress, but when it becomes excessive and interferes with daily life, it may be an anxiety disorder. According to the IMH National Youth Mental Health Study, 27% of Singapore youth experience severe anxiety, making it the most common mental health condition among young people.

What is Anxiety?
Anxiety is characterized by feelings of tension, worried thoughts, and physical changes like increased blood pressure. Everyone experiences anxiety from time to time, but anxiety disorders involve more than temporary worry or fear.

Common Symptoms of Anxiety:
Emotional Symptoms:
- Excessive worry or fear
- Feeling restless or on edge
- Irritability
- Difficulty concentrating
- Feeling overwhelmed
- Sense of impending doom or danger

Physical Symptoms:
- Rapid heartbeat or palpitations
- Sweating or trembling
- Shortness of breath
- Muscle tension
- Headaches
- Fatigue
- Insomnia
- Digestive issues

Types of Anxiety Disorders:
1. Generalized Anxiety Disorder (GAD) - Excessive worry about various aspects of life
2. Panic Disorder - Recurring panic attacks and fear of future attacks
3. Social Anxiety Disorder - Intense fear of social situations
4. Specific Phobias - Irrational fear of specific objects or situations
5. Separation Anxiety - Fear of being separated from attachment figures
6. Agoraphobia - Fear of places or situations where escape might be difficult

Causes of Anxiety:
- Genetics and family history
- Brain chemistry imbalances
- Environmental stressors
- Traumatic experiences
- Medical conditions
- Substance use
- Personality factors

Impact on Daily Life:
Anxiety can significantly affect:
- Academic or work performance
- Social relationships
- Physical health
- Sleep quality
- Decision-making abilities
- Overall quality of life

When to Seek Help:
Consider professional help if anxiety:
- Interferes with daily activities
- Causes significant distress
- Lasts for extended periods
- Includes panic attacks
- Leads to avoidance behaviors
- Affects relationships or work/school

Treatment Options:
- Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT)
- Exposure therapy
- Medications (if recommended by healthcare provider)
- Relaxation techniques
- Lifestyle modifications
- Support groups

Coping Strategies:
- Deep breathing exercises
- Progressive muscle relaxation
- Regular exercise
- Adequate sleep
- Limiting caffeine
- Mindfulness and meditation
- Social support

Remember: Anxiety is treatable. With appropriate support and treatment, people with anxiety disorders can manage their symptoms and live full, productive lives.
