Stress is the body's natural response to challenges or demands. While some stress can be motivating, chronic or excessive stress can have serious impacts on mental and physical health. According to the IMH NYMHS study, 12.9% of Singapore youth experience severe stress.

What is Stress?
Stress occurs when we perceive that demands placed on us exceed our ability to cope. It triggers a complex response involving our thoughts, emotions, behaviors, and physical reactions.

Types of Stress:
1. Acute Stress - Short-term stress from immediate pressures
2. Episodic Acute Stress - Frequent episodes of acute stress
3. Chronic Stress - Long-term stress from ongoing situations

Common Stress Symptoms:
Physical Symptoms:
- Headaches and muscle tension
- Fatigue and low energy
- Changes in appetite
- Sleep disturbances
- Frequent illnesses
- Digestive issues
- High blood pressure

Emotional Symptoms:
- Anxiety and worry
- Irritability and anger
- Sadness or depression
- Feeling overwhelmed
- Low motivation
- Restlessness

Behavioral Symptoms:
- Changes in eating habits
- Procrastination or neglect of responsibilities
- Social withdrawal
- Increased use of alcohol, drugs, or tobacco
- Nervous habits (nail biting, pacing)

Cognitive Symptoms:
- Racing thoughts
- Forgetfulness
- Difficulty concentrating
- Poor judgment
- Pessimistic thinking

Common Stress Triggers:
- Academic or work pressures
- Financial difficulties
- Relationship problems
- Major life changes
- Health issues
- Family responsibilities
- Social media and technology overload
- Uncertainty about the future

Effects of Chronic Stress:
Long-term stress can lead to:
- Mental health disorders (anxiety, depression)
- Cardiovascular disease
- Weakened immune system
- Digestive problems
- Sleep disorders
- Cognitive impairment
- Relationship difficulties

Healthy Stress Management:
1. Identify Stress Sources - Recognize what triggers your stress
2. Develop Coping Strategies - Learn healthy ways to manage stress
3. Build Resilience - Strengthen your ability to bounce back
4. Seek Support - Connect with others for help and understanding
5. Practice Self-Care - Prioritize your physical and mental well-being

Stress Management Techniques:
- Deep breathing and relaxation exercises
- Regular physical exercise
- Adequate sleep (7-9 hours for adults)
- Healthy eating habits
- Time management and organization
- Mindfulness and meditation
- Social connections
- Hobbies and enjoyable activities
- Professional counseling when needed

When to Seek Professional Help:
Consider professional support if stress:
- Interferes with daily functioning
- Causes persistent physical symptoms
- Leads to unhealthy coping behaviors
- Affects relationships or work performance
- Includes thoughts of self-harm
- Doesn't improve with self-help strategies

Remember: Some stress is normal and can even be beneficial. The key is learning to manage stress effectively and knowing when to seek help. Professional support is available and can provide valuable tools for stress management.
