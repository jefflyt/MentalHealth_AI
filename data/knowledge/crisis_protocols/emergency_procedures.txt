STEP-BY-STEP EMERGENCY PROCEDURES:

For Active Suicidal Crisis:

1. IMMEDIATE RESPONSE (0-2 minutes):
   - Call 995 if person is in immediate danger
   - Do NOT leave the person alone
   - Remove any potential means of self-harm
   - Speak calmly: "I'm here to help you. You're safe right now."

2. WHILE WAITING FOR HELP (2-15 minutes):
   - Continue talking calmly and reassuringly
   - Listen without judging or giving advice
   - Validate their pain: "This must be incredibly difficult"
   - Avoid saying: "Things will get better" or "Others have it worse"
   - Keep them talking about anything that engages them

3. WHEN HELP ARRIVES (15+ minutes):
   - Provide clear, factual information to responders
   - Share specific statements about self-harm
   - Mention any relevant medical/psychiatric history
   - Offer to accompany if appropriate and welcomed

For Psychotic Emergency:

1. SAFETY FIRST:
   - Call 995 immediately
   - Do not approach if person seems threatening
   - Clear the area of other people if necessary
   - Speak slowly, clearly, and calmly

2. COMMUNICATION:
   - Use simple, concrete language
   - Avoid arguing with delusions or hallucinations
   - Don't play along with false beliefs
   - Stay calm even if person becomes agitated

3. WAIT FOR PROFESSIONALS:
   - Do not try to restrain unless imminent danger
   - Maintain safe distance if person is agitated
   - Provide information to responders about observed behavior

For Substance-Related Emergency:

1. IMMEDIATE ACTIONS:
   - Call 995 for suspected overdose
   - Check breathing and consciousness
   - If unconscious, place in recovery position
   - Do not induce vomiting unless specifically instructed

2. INFORMATION GATHERING:
   - Try to determine what substance was used
   - Note time of ingestion if known
   - Save containers or remaining substances for medical team
   - Monitor vital signs if trained to do so

HOSPITAL EMERGENCY DEPARTMENT PROCEDURES:

What to Expect at IMH Emergency:
- Triage assessment within 15 minutes
- Mental health evaluation by psychiatric team
- Possible medical clearance if needed
- Safety assessment and risk evaluation
- Disposition planning (discharge, admission, referral)

Information to Provide:
- Specific statements about self-harm or suicide
- Timeline of current crisis
- Recent stressors or triggers
- Current medications
- Previous psychiatric history
- Substance use information
- Support system availability

Bringing Someone to Emergency:
- Call ahead if possible: 6389-2222
- Bring identification and insurance information
- List of current medications
- Emergency contact information
- Stay with person during intake if welcomed

MOBILE CRISIS TEAM SERVICES:

When Available:
- Some areas have mobile crisis teams
- Can respond to homes, schools, workplaces
- Provide on-site assessment and intervention
- Help avoid unnecessary hospitalization

How to Access:
- Call IMH main number for availability: 6389-2000
- Some services available through polyclinics
- Private healthcare may offer mobile services

SPECIAL SITUATIONS:

Child/Adolescent Emergency:
- Contact parents/guardians immediately
- Consider school involvement if applicable
- Pediatric emergency services may be needed
- Child protection services if abuse suspected

Elderly Emergency:
- Consider medical complications
- Assess for cognitive impairment
- Evaluate caregiver stress factors
- May need geriatric psychiatry consultation

Language Barriers:
- Request interpreter services
- Use translation apps if necessary
- Find bilingual family member if appropriate
- IMH has interpreter services available

Legal Considerations:
- Involuntary commitment may be necessary
- Police may assist with transport if needed
- Mental Health Act provisions may apply
- Document all actions taken

PREVENTION AND PREPARATION:

Emergency Contact Cards:
Create wallet-sized cards with:
- Emergency services: 995
- IMH Emergency: 6389-2222
- Crisis hotlines: SOS 1767, SAMH 1800-283-7019
- Personal emergency contacts
- Current medications and medical conditions

Family/Support System Education:
- Teach warning signs recognition
- Practice emergency procedures
- Know how to remove means of self-harm
- Understand when to call for help
- Have emergency numbers readily available

Environmental Safety:
- Secure medications in locked box
- Remove or lock firearms
- Install barriers on high places if needed
- Ensure sharp objects are secured
- Create calm, supportive environment
